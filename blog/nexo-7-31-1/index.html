NEXO 7.31.1: provider circuit breaker and reliability hardening

June 10, 2026. This patch release is the Brain half of the Phase 1 reliability program: automations stop losing work when the engine behind them is temporarily unavailable.

What changed

  • Provider circuit breaker. When Claude or Codex is unavailable — credits exhausted, rate limited, auth expired — headless automations (email monitor, deep sleep, evolution, follow-ups…) no longer launch sessions that die mid-flight and burn their retries. Work pauses and queues, one probe per retry window re-tests, and everything resumes automatically when the engine is back.
  • One notice, in your language, signed by your agent. Instead of a per-item escalation storm (previously in English regardless of configuration), the operator gets a single message when automations pause and another when they resume.
  • Protocol nudge shaping (shadow). The "non-trivial work without an open task" reminder gains a streak threshold, a cooldown and headless awareness — running in shadow/log-only mode to calibrate with real data before activation.
  • Valid client config push. The sync no longer writes an invalid mcp__* permission rule to Claude Code settings (the cause of a Settings Warning on every launch) and repairs installs that already had it; Codex config writes are now change-only, avoiding repeated trust prompts.

Why it matters

An agent platform is only as reliable as its behaviour when things go wrong. With 7.31.1 a missing credit top-up degrades to a quiet pause-and-resume instead of lost work and inbox noise — and the operator hears about it exactly once, from their agent, in their language.
