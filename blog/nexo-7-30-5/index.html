NEXO 7.30.5 - Morning preparation

v7.30.5 is a focused Brain patch over v7.30.4. It changes the morning automation from a configurable briefing list into a start-of-day preparation assistant: what changed, what matters, what can block the user, and what should happen next.

The new preference schema adds automatic relevance, changes since yesterday, next actions, relevant public context, interest selection, excluded topics, and plain-language help metadata. NEXO can adapt from the user's profile, recent work, agenda, emails, tasks, and saved preferences without asking the user to choose a manual role.

Public headlines are now treated as contextual signals, not a generic news block. Brain builds internal interest/location/profile queries, filters excluded topics, deduplicates headlines, and includes them only when they are useful for the user's day. Users do not paste RSS URLs or manage source plumbing.

The preference catalog also exposes the new options through chat, including Spanish aliases for interests, exclusions, length, tone, and delivery. That lets a user's agent explain or change Morning preparation settings conversationally instead of forcing non-technical users into Preferences.
